Legal Requirements of Digital Projects

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) is an EU measure introduced in 2018. It is concerned with how organisations use individuals’ personal data and has been designed to give people greater control of their data. Non-compliance will result in hefty fines as BA have found after a data breach which will cost them up to €200 million!

At the heart of the regulation are 8 basic individual rights:

  1. The right to access

    2. This means that individuals have the right to request access to their personal data and to ask how their data is used by the company after it has been gathered. The company must provide a copy of the personal data, free of charge and in electronic format if requested.

  2. The right to be forgotten

    3. if consumers are no longer customers, or if they withdraw their consent from a company to use their personal data, then they have the right to have their data deleted.

  3. The right to data portability

    4. Individuals have a right to transfer their data from one service provider to another. And it must happen in a commonly used and machine readable format.

  4. The right to be informed

    5. This covers any gathering of data by companies, and individuals must be informed before data is gathered. Consumers have to opt in for their data to be gathered, and consent must be freely given rather than implied.

  5. The right to have information corrected

    6. this ensures that individuals can have their data updated if it is out of date or incomplete or incorrect.

  6. The right to restrict processing

    7. Individuals can request that their data is not used for processing. Their record can remain in place, but not be used.

  7. The right to object

    8. This includes the right of individuals to stop the processing of their data for direct marketing. There are no exemptions to this rule, and any processing must stop as soon as the request is received. In addition, this right must be made clear to individuals at the very start of any communication.

  8. The right to be notified

    9. If there has been a data breach which compromises an individual’s personal data, the individual has a right to be informed within 72 hours of first having become aware of the breach

When planning digital projects, ensure the following is done:

  1. Map the data flows of within an organisation and the proposed digital project
  2. Determine data you need to keep
  3. Insert security measures
  4. Review documentation
  5. Establish procedures for handling personal data
  6. Determine a communication plan in case of a data breach

Even though GDPR may seem like a headache for most digital projects, it can also present opportunities. Demonstrate you care about individuals privacy and show this in product design and delivery.
