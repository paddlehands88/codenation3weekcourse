Choice of Systems and Software

When choosing IT systems and software the following process can be followed:

  1. Define your Needs

    • Define what problems you want your IT systems and software to solve
    • Further define this list into ‘must have’ and ‘would like to have’

  2. Establish a Shortlist

    • Search market / websites and make a list of those providers that meet your requirements
    • List pro’s and con’s of each

  3. Understand the Pricing

    • The cheapest is not always the best. Ask which systems//software offers the best VALUE
    • Examine vendors pricing policy - What is free and what is paid for?
    • Understand the anticipated product life span and the anticipated maintenance costs.

  4. Ask for a Demo

    5. For IT systems look to speak with a vendor’s previous customers. Give them a call or better still, visit their premises and speak with end users face to face. Schedule a demo with each vendor and have a list of predefined questions you would like answering. Some essential questions would include:

    • Can the software integrate smoothly with existing systems?
    • Can the software be customised?
    • What support is there if I have issues needed to be resolved quickly?
    • What is the process for software updates and upgrades?

  5. Make a Choice

    6. When making a decision to go ahead, ensure you are not only comfortable with the IT systems and software, but also the vendor too. You want to begin a relationship with the vendor whereby you can call upon them for all your future IT requirements. If you are not comfortable with the vendor, carry on searching.
