The Purpose of IT in Modern Business


IT is essential to the vast majority of modern businesses. Whether this is a Sole Trader such as a local decorator, to large multinational corporations like Google, IT is essential. For example, a local decorator could have a website created to advertise their business, explain how they work, show their prices to prospective customers and display images of completed decorating jobs. They will likely use accounting software, such as Quickbooks, to complete and file their annual accounts.

At the other end of the spectrum large corporations will likely have IT departments that service all IT related issues with other departments. They will have an IT strategy to compliment their corporate/business strategy.

When devising a strategy, an organisation will look for opportunities that are presented in their business environments. Business environment factors include:

  • Political
  • Economical
  • Social
  • Technological
  • Legislative
  • Ecological

Of all these factors the most dynamic is ‘technology.’ Moore’s Law, whereby processing power capabilities of computer chips doubled every year. (This has since been revised to approximately doubling every two years) When technological advancements are made, this changes the business landscape which presents windows of opportunity for organisations to exploit. A good example is mapping software which utilises satellite navigation and real time traffic data to determine the quickest routes for drivers. In London, black cab drivers are required to complete ‘The Knowledge’ where they spend a number of months driving around London to learn where all streets and roads are, and the best routes to take. This is a costly activity. Uber was originally set up in 2009 (under the name Uber Cab) and through combining a host of technological advancements (Smartphones, GPS etc) Uber has gained a strong market share in the Greater London market to the dismay of Black Cab Drivers.

In conclusion, companies have to adapt to their changing environment, and if they don’t, their competitive advantages will erode eventually leading to business failure.
